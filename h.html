Element Symbol: H

Atomic Number:

1 (the first element in the periodic table) Atomic Mass: 1.008 u (unified atomic mass unit) Appearance: Hydrogen is a colorless, odorless, and tasteless gas at room temperature.

Classification:

Type: Nonmetal Category: Diatomic gas (H₂) It’s the lightest and most abundant element in the universe. Electron Configuration: 1s¹ (one electron in the 1s orbital)

Physical Properties:

Boiling Point: −252.87°C (−423.17°F) Melting Point: −259.16°C (−434.49°F) Density: 0.08988 g/L at 0°C and 1 atm Chemical Properties: Highly reactive: Forms compounds with most elements, such as water (H₂O), methane (CH₄), and hydrogen chloride (HCl).

Isotopes:

Protium (¹H): Most common, no neutrons. Deuterium (²H): Contains one neutron (heavy hydrogen). Tritium (³H): Contains two neutrons, radioactive. Occurrence: It is abundant in the universe, especially in stars (including the Sun) where it undergoes nuclear fusion. On Earth, it’s found mainly in compounds like water (H₂O) and hydrocarbons.
